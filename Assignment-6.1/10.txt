edt july in a meeting at ferraris maranello base which is attended by bernie ecclestone the teams agree to form a new association
the formula one teams association fota will work with the fia and formula one management to come up with a new concorde agreement
september fota is officially launched with the ferrari president luca di montezemolo named as first chairman and the toyota president john howett as vice chairman
three separate commissions are set up to look at sporting technical and commercial rules
october the fia president max mosley meets di montezemolo and howett in geneva and agrees that the sport must make dramatic cost cuts
december fota finalises its plans for cost cutting measures and engine rules having been asked by the fia to make proposals
fota also raises the issue of teams receiving a greater share of the sports profits an aspect backed by the fia as it bids to secure the future of the smaller teams
december the fia and fota agree a series of cost cutting measures their monaco meeting being hailed as the most successful meeting on formula one matters which any of the participants can remember
december the world motor sport council wmsc rubber stamps the measures which include a ban on in season testing a reduction in aerodynamic development and the doubling of engine life from 
january mosley writes to fota asking the teams to make sure these rules which the teams believe will mean budgets cut by % are implemented ahead of a fota meeting
march di montezemolo says teams are ready to sign a new concorde agreement until end of but fota calls for an immediate change to points system
march the wmsc announces technical regulations for that will force teams to either adhere to a £m budget cap and have technical freedom or run with the current regulations and with financial freedom
it also rejects fotas points proposal and says the champion will be the driver with most race wins
march fota hits out at the new rules which it says would create a two tier system but claims it is not opposed to a budget cap
may following strained relations caused by the diffuser row in the opening grands prix fota meets to secure its unity and requests urgent talks with the fia on the rule changes
may toyota followed by ferrari renault and red bull say they will not enter the championship unless changes are made to the two tier budget cap system
may fota meets mosley and ecclestone but the parties fail to reach an agreement over the budget cap
may ferrari attempts to gain an injunction against the fias regulations through the french courts but it is rejected
may williams breaks the ranks by lodging an unconditional application for entry and is subsequently suspended by fota
may the remaining current teams submit entries on the condition that the regulations are based on rules that are identical for all competitors and amended in accordance with proposals fota has submitted to the fia
june force india announces that its entry is unconditional and is expelled by fota the remaining eight teams meet their drivers two days later and receive their support in attempt to scrap the budget cap
june fia publishes entry list in which it says red bull toro rosso and ferrari have unconditional entries while the remaining five fota teams are asked to drop their conditions if they are to race
june financial representatives from the fia and fota fail to reach an agreement the fia saying its plans for a £m budget cap will go ahead and later accusing fota of trying to take over the regulation of formula one
june a day ahead of the deadline for unconditionally entering the championship fota announces it is to set up a breakaway series the fia responds by saying it cannot permit a financial arms race in the championship
june the fia and fota agree there will be no parallel championship next season following talks in paris to avert the threatened breakaway
as a result of the peace deal max mosley says he will not seek to extend his time in office
